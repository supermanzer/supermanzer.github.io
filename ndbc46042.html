NDBC 46042 - Regional Montery Bay

For much of the oceanographic research done on the Monterey Bay, the regional National Data Buoy Center (NDBC) Buoy 46042 has been used. Located 27 nautical miles west northwest (WNW) of the city of Monterey, this buoy measures the regional oceanic conditions near the mouth of the Monterey Bay. My own research has relied upon it greatly and I hope to use my familiarity with this data as a fertile source for developing interactive data visualizations.

Upwelling

Since much of the interest in the Monterey Region is due to the very productive nearshore ecosystem, which is in turn due to the upwelling centers north and south of the Monterey Bay, much attention is paid to whether or not wind conditions are upwelling favorable.
